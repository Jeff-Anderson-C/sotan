Creation

Collaboration is best used in the menu creation process. You have a concept, or feeling for how you want your menu to read and perform. We can work through the details and develop a menu that brings your vision to the table. We will engineer the menu to provide balance in the kitchen during peak hours and on the p&l at the end of the period. Menus can begin to drift away from your core concept over time, most of the time it's in an effort to provide more/new guests with an option that wasn't present before, and sometimes it's to save cost. We can focus on your strengths and produce a menu that's efficient and representative.

Product

Keeping track of food cost is a never ending battle. We can further develop your current routine to provide you with more detailed information, and solutions for when variances arise. Accurately costing menus and recipes is one of the simplest, though tedious, steps to take towards realizing your true food cost. Let's clean up order guides, document our specs, and get into a routine of checking in on our costs. Having a good understanding of where you food cost is and how to adjust it on the fly is a key to offering a menu at a price that's fair to you and your guest.

Why work with a consultant?

You and your staff have a full week already, but there are certain tasks that you would like to make routine; costing your menu is a big one. Not only does this process take time but: the learning curve can be intensive, knowing where to start can be difficult, and figuring out how to complete the process as efficiently as possible may be enough to have you convince yourself it's not worth the time. You don't want to pull your chef off the line to focus on admin when you have guests to serve, and you'd rather not absorb another full time salary to get these projects accomplished. You can benefit from the years of experience others have gathered in these specific areas; at a fraction of the cost of another annual salary. By establishing and monitoring your food cost, you're better equipped to make purchasing and pricing decisions that can save on cost of goods and increase menu item contribution.
